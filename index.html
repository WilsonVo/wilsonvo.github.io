hi, i'm

First-generation junior at Brown University studying computer cngineering, focused on digital design, verification, and embedded systems with experience in ASIC/FPGA design, image processing, and backend architecture. Skilled in Verilog, Java, C/C++, Matlab and Python. Passionate about building reliable, low-level systems with real-world impact.

I believe in socially responsible hardware design. As someone passionate about digital logic, ASICs, and FPGAs, I see hardware as the foundation of tomorrow's technology—but with that comes responsibility. It's not just about speed, power, or cost. It's about designing systems that are ethical, inclusive, and sustainable. Thoughtful engineering means asking not just how we build, but why—and who it impacts.

This site showcases who I am, the projects I've built, the communities I'm part of, and how you can get in touch.

about me

I grew up in Lawrence, Massachusetts after my parents immigrated from Vietnam in 1993. From a young age, I was captivated by computers—curious about how something so complex could be built from logic and electricity. In high school, I explored this curiosity through engineering, robotics, and computer science programs that introduced me to the computing systems.

At Brown, that early interest evolved into a deep passion for digital logic, hardware design, and embedded systems. I have had the opportunity to work on several hardware-centric projects, including designing a single-cycle RISC-V processor, a bomb diffusion game, and a stack-based calculator—all implemented in Verilog and deployed on FPGA boards. I even wrote the little mouse tracker Feel free to play with it! Currently, I am expanding my hardware design experience as a PCB designer for the BlindFind Project in the Laboratory for Engineering Man-Machine Systems (LEMS) at Brown.
